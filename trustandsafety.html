What are Zowasel's Standard Payment Terms?

Zowasel typically makes payment within 48 to 72 hours after the delivery confirmation by buyers.

To protect our sellers and buyers, plus ourselves, we follow the normal buyers' payment terms by making payments after delivery within 48 to 72 hours.

However, some buyers' typical payment terms are up to 30 to 60 days. Hence, sellers will be informed if payment will exceed the normal term, as stated, before supply.

Sellers are advised of every buyer's payment terms before offer acceptance and commodity shipment. Whatever the term, payment is guaranteed for every successful transaction on the Zowasel platform!

Am I expected to pay offloading fees when I ship my commodities to a buyer?

Buyers oversee offloading processes and provide sellers with warehouse results at no cost. Sellers are not responsible for paying to offload their trucks at the buyer's warehouse. After conducting a quality check, buyers oversee offloading processes and provide sellers with warehouse results at no cost.

N.B: Some buyers require payment for offloading.

Why does Zowasel operate an escrow account system?

We operate an escrow account system to protect our sellers and buyers from default.

To protect our sellers and buyers, even when a buyer pays ahead in full or deposit, Zowasel holds the payment in an escrow account and only makes payment to the seller as soon as the buyer confirms the waybill received per the buyer's payment terms.

Why does Zowasel sign contracts with buyers?

Zowasel signs fulfillment agreements with buyers to protect sellers on the Marketplace platform, after which the buyer issues payment domiciliation to join the Zowasel platform.

We match buyers and sellers of the same commodity. Decisions on pricing and terms are agreed on together. Zowasel then enforces smart contracts and ensures that all transactions are shipped and payments are made after delivery without issues.

Firstly, Zowasel signs fulfillment contracts with every buyer to insure against default and or delayed payments, plus payment domiciliation to our escrow accounts to protect our crop sellers.

This has the added benefit that Zowasel never takes a position opposite its customers – Zowasel does better when you do better.

Why does Zowasel make payment only after delivery confirmation?

To protect buyers and sellers, and ourselves, we follow the normal buyers' payment terms by making payments only after delivery.

We make payment after delivery confirmation or hold a reserve for a simple reason: we want to protect ourselves, the seller, and the buyer if the invoice is not paid in full or delivery is not made.

There are many reasons why invoices are not paid in full, including Order errors, Breakage (i.e., not all crops were delivered in good condition), delivery errors, quantity and quality errors, etc.).

By not paying for the invoice in full receivables, we protect ourselves and our customers. Bear in mind that Zowasel does hold the payment in an escrow account. We will release the payment as soon as the buyer confirms the waybill received.

Zowasel enforces smart contracts and ensures that all transactions are shipped and payments are made after delivery confirmation without any issues.
